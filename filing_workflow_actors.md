# RegReportX: Report Generation & Filing Workflow (Maker-Checker Principle)

This document outlines the step-by-step lifecycle of a regulatory report in **RegReportX**, tailored for RBI (Reserve Bank of India) standards. It defines the exact actors responsible for each phase to ensure compliance with the mandatory **Maker-Checker (Four-Eyes) principle**.

## 1. Draft Phase
*   **Actor:** **Reporting Officer** (The "Maker")
*   **Action:** The Reporting Officer logs into the **Reporting Workspace**. They select a regulatory template (e.g., LCR or Form A), set the reporting period (e.g., Q1), and click "Generate Draft Report." The system aggregates all the prepared and cleansed data from the underlying tables into a draft format. This forms the baseline structure of the report before final risk metrics are applied.

## 2. Risk Calculation Phase
*   **Actor:** **Risk Analyst** 
*   **Action:** Once the initial draft is generated by the Reporting Officer, the **Risk Analyst** steps in. They review the aggregated draft data and trigger the Risk Engine to compute critical regulatory metrics like Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA), Capital to Risk-Weighted Assets Ratio (CRAR), and Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR). These calculated `RiskMetric` and `ExposureRecord` values are then injected into the draft report.

## 3. Validate Phase
*   **Actor:** **Compliance Analyst** 
*   **Action:** After the Risk Analyst finishes computing the necessary risk metrics against the draft, the report is essentially fully populated with all required data. 
    *   The **Compliance Analyst** clicks "Validate Report." This action triggers a final schema check against the RBI's requirements (e.g., ensuring the XML structure perfectly matches the expected format, verifying that no mandatory fields are blank, and ensuring mathematical totals reconcile). 
    *   **Status Change:** Once validation passes successfully, the system automatically transitions the `RegReport` status from **"Draft"** to **"Validated"** (or **"Under Review"**). This flags to the Senior Managers that the report is complete, internally consistent, and ready for official approval.
    *   *Note:* If the validation throws a critical data error at this late stage, an **Operations Officer** may be pulled in to manually override or correct the underlying data anomalies via the Exceptions module, but the Compliance Analyst is the primary driver of this validation step.

## 3. Approve Phase
*   **Actor:** **Risk Analyst** OR a **Senior Reporting/Compliance Manager** (The "Checker")
*   **Action:** A different, authorized user *must* perform this step to satisfy RBI audit requirements (Maker-Checker). This secondary, senior actor logs into the system, reviews the drafted report alongside any attached `Evidence` (such as an Operations Officer's formal explanation for a manual data override), and clicks "Approve." 
    *   **Impact:** This action permanently locks the internal data associated with this report instance so it cannot be altered further.

## 4. File / Export Phase
*   **Actor:** **Reporting Officer**
*   **Action:** Now that the report is locked and approved by the Checker, the Reporting Officer clicks "Export." They download the final, validated `.xml`, `.xbrl`, or `.csv` file to their secure local machine. They then open a web browser, log into the RBI's external CIMS (Centralised Information Management System) portal, and manually upload that file. Finally, they return to RegReportX and click "Mark as Filed" to close out the workflow for that period.

## 5. Audit Logging & System Locks Phase
*   **Actor:** **The System (Automated)**
*   **Action:** As soon as the "Approve" and "Mark as Filed" actions are triggered, the RegReportX backend automatically creates immutable, indelible records in the `AuditLog` table. This automated process stamps the exact timestamp, the action taken, and the precise `UserID`s of both the Maker and the Checker, ensuring full traceability for future regulatory inspections.
